Business Scenario

D&K Books Pty Ltd is a bookstore owned by Mr. Dean Kerr. The business occupies two levels of an office building connected by escalators and lifts. D&K Books employs approximately six sales staff, one operation manager, two administrative officers, a bookkeeper and a marketing manager. They have an Ethernet network consisting of ten PCs (Intel I3 Desktop cloned), two switches, a router and three printers. They use the QuickBooks software to manage their entire business, including sales, inventory, ordering, accounts receivable, accounts payable, payroll and employee management. They also have two EFTPOS terminals one on each floor.

Support Areas

Stakeholders

The Business Owner (Mr. Dean Kerr)

As the owner of D&K Books, Mr. Dean Kerr has a substantial investment in the company and is deeply committed to its success and financial growth.

Operation Manager

The operation manager is responsible for overseeing the daily activities of the bookstore, playing a vital role in ensuring that different processes are efficient and effective.

Sales Staff

The sales team plays a crucial role in determining the success of the company, as they are responsible for generating sales and ensuring customer satisfaction.

Administrative Officers

The efficiency of administrative officers plays a crucial role in ensuring the smooth operation of a business as they are responsible for managing a range of administrative tasks.

Bookkeeper

The bookkeeper's key responsibility is to effectively manage financial records, as it directly affects financial reporting and decision-making processes.

Marketing Manager

The marketing manager's efforts in promoting the bookstore and attracting customers are crucial in determining the business's visibility and sales.

Suppliers

The suppliers of books and other products have a significant impact on the bookstore's inventory and customer satisfaction. They are crucial in delivering high-quality products within the specified timeframe, making them important stakeholders in the business.

Customers

The success of D&K Books is directly influenced by the satisfaction and preferences of its customers, who are external stakeholders. This encompasses both online customers who browse the website and those who visit the physical store.



Questions

  • Relationship between data, information and knowledge
  • Quantitative data
  • Qualitative data
  • Quantitative + Qualitative

Data refers to unprocessed alphanumeric values obtained through various acquisition methods. In their most basic form, data comprises raw alphanumeric values. Conversely, information is the product of processing, organizing, or structuring data to provide context and significance. Essentially, information is the outcome of processed data. Knowledge, however, represents our comprehension. It is unique to each individual and is influenced by their past experiences and insights, which shape how they interpret and assign meaning to information. To translate knowledge into action, an individual must possess the authority and capability to make and implement decisions. Both knowledge and authority are necessary to generate actionable information that can have an impact.

Quantitative data is numerical information, including counts and numbers, that has a unique value for each data set. Researchers can use this data for mathematical computations and statistical analysis, which allows them to make informed decisions in real-life situations based on these mathematical conclusions. Quantitative research is the process of collecting and analyzing numerical data. It can be used to find patterns and averages, make predictions, test causal relationships, and generalize results to wider populations.

Qualitative data refers to non-numerical information that provides researchers with a detailed and nuanced understanding of human experiences, behaviors, perceptions, and attitudes. Unlike quantitative data, which focuses on measurable factors, qualitative data explores the essence and complexity of phenomena through descriptive and interpretive methods. You would use qualitative data to help answer questions like who your customers are, what issues or problems they're facing, and where they need to focus their attention, so you can better solve those issues.

The sequential exploratory design approach involves first collecting and analyzing qualitative data, followed by using quantitative data to confirm or authenticate the qualitative findings. For instance, a study on the difficulties encountered by low-income families could use interviews to gather qualitative data on the barriers to healthcare access, and then use surveys to obtain quantitative data on the frequency of these obstacles.

When conducting quality assurance for web design, it is essential to take into account the specific needs and preferences of the user, which are known as user requirements. The process of gathering user requirements involves utilizing different techniques like surveys, interviews, and focus groups. After identifying the user requirements, it is crucial to create a website requirements document that encompasses a checklist of acceptance criteria for each requirement.

Client Requirements


INTERVIEWS

Conduct in-depth interviews with key stakeholders to gather insights into their business objectives, values, and expectations.

SURVEYS AND QUESTIONNAIRES

Create surveys to collect specific information about the client's preferences, target audience, and desired features.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

Analyze competitors' websites to identify industry standards, trends, and potential areas for differentiation.



USER RESEARCH

Develop user personas based on research to understand the target audience's needs, preferences, and behaviors.

STAKEHOLDER WORKSHOPS

Organize workshops or brainstorming sessions with key stakeholders to collaboratively define goals, features, and priorities.

SOCIAL MEDIA ANALYSIS

Review the client's social media presence and online reputation to ensure consistency in branding and messaging.



INDUSTRY RESEARCH

Conduct research on industry-specific design trends, user expectations, and best practices.

FEEDBACK SESSIONS

Establish a feedback loop with the client throughout the design process, using prototypes or wireframes to gather input and make adjustments.

ACCESSIBILITY AND COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

Ensure awareness of and adherence to accessibility standards and legal requirements related to the client's industry.
